Repeat Defect Definition

A repeat defect exists when the same aircraft experiences the same component fault, or a defect in the same ATA chapter, within the preceding thirty days. Reliability analysts should distinguish repeated troubleshooting from independent events.

Escalation Threshold

Three similar defects on one aircraft within sixty days should be presented at the fleet reliability review with engineering action owner, open tasks, and material support status.

Vendor Monitoring

Track component and vendor concentrations separately. A high component count with dispersed vendors indicates system susceptibility, while one vendor concentration suggests supply quality or modification effectiveness issues.

Rectification Performance

Mean rectification time should be reviewed alongside AOG count because low closure time on non-dispatch items can hide poor operational impact from a smaller number of severe events.

Spares Coordination

Before recommending reorder quantities, compare average monthly usage against current repeat defect activity, open deferred items, and lead-time exposure for critical components.

Engineering Action Tracking

Recommended actions should move from monitoring to escalation when severity, recurrence, and spares exposure align. Document the rationale in daily control tower handover notes.
